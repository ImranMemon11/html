Paksaaf🌎

"Turning Pakistan’s waste into health & wealth"

    Executive💡Summary

  • Paksaaf wants to change how Pakistan handles waste by recycling and sorting it better. Our plan helps protect the environment, improve health, and create new jobs. We will work with homes, businesses, local governments, and schools to collect waste. We will earn money through waste collection plans, selling recycled materials, and making compost. In 5 years, we hope to work in many cities, reduce landfill waste, train workers, and build a recycling marketplace in Pakistan.

What Problem Are We Solving?

Pakistan generates tons of solid waste, which has been increasing more than 2.4 percent annually. Similar to many other developing nations, Pakistan faces a challenge in terms of waste management infrastructure, resulting in significant environmental issues but waste can be business opportunities

  1. Pakistan makes over 3 million tons of waste every year.
  2. Less than 50% of this waste is properly collected or recycled, leading to widespread mismanagement.
  3. A lot of waste is thrown in open areas, which pollutes air, water, and land.
  4. This causes health problems for people, like spreading diseases and attracting pest.
  5. Useful materials are wasted, leading to lost chances to earn money and create jobs.

Our Solution :

  1. Paksaaf collects and recycles waste using smart technology.
  2. Turns garbage into useful products, energy, and raw materials.
  3. Teaches people how to manage waste in a better, cleaner way.
  4. Helps protect the environment by reusing waste instead of throwing it away.
  5. Gives smart bins (dustbin) to help people sort waste at home or work.

Target Customers :

  1. Urban households and housing societies.
  2. Restaurants, hotels, and offices.
  3. Local municipalities and schools.
  4. Small recycling companies that need sorted waste.

Revenue Model :

  1. Weekly waste pickup subscriptions for steady income.
  2. Selling sorted recyclables to manufacturers for profit.
  3. Making and selling compost from organic waste.
  4. Partnering with government/NGOs for funding and projects.
